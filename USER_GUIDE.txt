LOOM – User Guide

Overview

LOOM is a gesture-controlled Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) that enables music composition through a virtual theremin. Utilizing hand tracking powered by MediaPipe, you can perform melodies without a physical controller.

The application is organized into five main sections:

1. Camera
2. Transport
3. Theremin Controls
4. Tracks
5. Timeline

Camera

The Camera window shows the live webcam feed and tracks your dominant hand. Before using LOOM:

- Allow camera access when prompted.
- Ensure your hand is clearly visible.
- Use adequate lighting.
- Keep your hand in the central area of the image for optimal tracking.

Gesture Mapping

- Move left/right: Change pitch
- Move up/down: Change volume
- Adjust the number of extended fingers: Switch instrument

Theremin Controls

This panel allows you to configure the virtual instrument. Depending on the version in use, you can:

- View the active instrument.
- Monitor the generated note and current volume.
- Adjust theremin parameters.
- Select different sound presets.

Altering the number of visible fingers will automatically switch the active instrument.

Transport

The Transport panel manages playback and recording.

- Play: Starts playback of your current project.
- Stop: Stops playback and resets the playhead.
- Record: Starts recording your theremin performance.

Upon finishing recording, LOOM automatically creates a clip and places it on the selected track.

Tracks

The Tracks window oversees all project tracks. Each track can be:

- Renamed
- Deleted
- Muted
- Set to solo mode
- Armed for recording (if enabled)

Tracks can contain multiple clips.

Timeline

The Timeline is where you assemble your project. Here you can:

- View all recorded clips.
- Position and move clips in time.
- Select clips.
- Arrange multiple tracks.
- Track the playback cursor during playback.

Each horizontal lane represents a track.

Recording Workflow

1. Launch LOOM.
2. Allow camera access.
3. Create a new track.
4. Select the track.
5. Press Record.
6. Perform using hand gestures.
7. Press Stop.
8. A new clip appears automatically in the Timeline.
9. Repeat to build your composition.

Playback

Press Play to listen to your project. During playback:

- The playhead moves across the timeline.
- Clips play in chronological order.
- Muted tracks are ignored.
- Solo tracks take precedence over others.

Export

LOOM allows you to export your work in formats such as WAV, MP3, MIDI, or as a project file. Choose your desired format from the Export menu.

Tips

- Work in a well-lit environment.
- Keep your hand inside the camera frame.
- Avoid quick movements when learning controls.
- Start with short clips.
- Build songs layer by layer.
- Utilize multiple tracks for different instruments.

Troubleshooting

Camera issues:
- Check camera permissions.
- Ensure no other application is using the webcam.

Hand not detected:
- Improve lighting.
- Keep your entire hand visible and centered in the camera frame.

No sound:
- Verify system volume settings.
- Confirm that the browser or application has audio permissions and playback is started.

Typical Workflow

1. Open LOOM
2. Allow Camera Access
3. Create Track
4. Choose Instrument
5. Record Performance
6. Clip Appears
7. Arrange Timeline
8. Play Project
9. Export
